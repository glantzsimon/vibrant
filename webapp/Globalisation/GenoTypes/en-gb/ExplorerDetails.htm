Muscular, quirky and adventurous, the Explorer is a biological problem-solver with an impressive ability to adapt to environmental changes and a better-than-average capacity for gene repair.

The Explorer’s vulnerability to hormonal imbalances and brain strain can be overcome with a balanced diet and lifestyle.

Slogan: I'll do it my way!

Explorer Strengths

  • Lateral thinker conerned with changing concepts and perception
  • "Visual simultaneous" - scan multiple sensory inputs simultaneously
  • Great entrepreneur
  • High IQ
  • Good gene repair
  • Recovers quickly from illness
  • Physical stamina
  • Great memory retention into advanced age
  • Effective problem solver
  • Ages gracefully

Explorer Vulnerabilities

  • Often borderline anaemic
  • Often Hypersensitive to chemicals
  • Sensitive to caffeine
  • Accident prone
  • Liver detoxification can be ineffective
  • Medically hard to diagnose
  • Tendency for blood irregularities
  • Risk of type 1 diabetes
  • Risk of autism, epilepsy or dyslexia
  • Pront to food and environmental allergies
  • Yeast infections

Exercise

It should be emphasized that the one condition of all exercise for Explorers is the breaking of a sweat. If you are an Explorer and you do not sweat when you exercise, you are not exercising enough! To stay fit and healthy, reduce stress, and increase endurance, you’ll need about 40 minutes of exercise 4–5 times weekly. Be sure to warm up for at least 5–10 minutes with some gentle stretching before beginning any aerobic activity.
