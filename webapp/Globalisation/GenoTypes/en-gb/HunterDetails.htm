Hunters are often taller than average, slender and intense, with an overabundance of adrenaline and a fierce, nervous energy that calms down with age.

Hunters are vulnerable to systemic burnout when overstressed and their modern challenge is to conserve energy for the long haul.

Slogan: shoot first, ask questins later!

Hunter Strengths

  • Logical
  • Detail-oriented
  • Handles stress well when well-rested and well-nourished
  • Tends towards symmetry
  • High energy levels when in balance
  • Efficient metabolism when in balance
  • Athletic
  • Strong bones and tissues

Hunter Vulnerabilities

  • Prone to burnout when overworked and inadequately nourished
  • Vulnerable to digestive issues
  • Vulnerable to inflammation
  • Pront to aging rapidly if out of balance
  • Allergies
  • Autoimmune diseases
  • Depression
  • Joint problems
  • Celiac disease
  • Reproductive cancers (with age and when out of balance)

Exercise

Hunter GenoTypes need regular, vigorous exercise to stay fit and healthy, reduce stress, and increase endurance. To accomplish this, about 40 minutes of exercise daily, or most days is ideal. It's important to warm up for at least 5–10 minutes with some gentle stretching before beginning any aerobic activity.
