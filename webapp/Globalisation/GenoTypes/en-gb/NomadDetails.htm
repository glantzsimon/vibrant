Type 6 - Nomad

Nomad - a genetic profile of extremes, with a great sensitivity to environmental conditions—especially changes in altitude and barometric pressure. The Nomad is vulnerable to neuromuscular and immune problems. Yet a well-conditioned Nomad has the enviable gift of controlling calorie intake and aging gracefully.

Slogan: a new career in a new town

  • Often quiet but witty
  • Generally optimistic

  • Very good at using visulisation for health and recovery

Vulnerabilities

  • Tends to keep emotions hidden
  • Extremes and variables make diagnosis often challenging
  • Sensitive digestive tract

  • They have a higher incidence of autoimmune diseases like lupus, multiple sclerosis, and ALS.
  • Problem areas for Nomads include the liver and spleen, leading to inflammatory liver disease, hepatitis, and cirrhosis.

Metabolic Profile

  • Nomads typically have large bones, with BMI and waist-to-hip ratios often higher due to muscularity rather than excess fat.
  • They are commonly mesomorphs, with low to medium body fat, medium to large bone size, and a medium to high metabolism.
  • Tall Nomads usually have broad, balanced facial features, while shorter ones have rounder jawlines.
  • Fetal development of Nomads is influenced by altitude, with those in high-altitude areas tending to be taller and more symmetrical.
  • Nomads often exhibit distinctive fingerprint patterns, including an abundance of ulnar loop patterns.
  • Shorter Nomads may show asymmetry, such as differences in breast size for women and testicle size for men.
  • Taller Nomads tend to be more symmetrical, indicating favorable conditions in the womb.
  • Nomads with a high count of ulnar loop patterns and a family history of Alzheimer's disease may benefit from cognitive support as outlined in the Nomad Diet Plan.

Immune System Profile

  • Nomads, early herders, experienced rapid and continuous migration, exposing them to various climates and microbes.
  • The adoption of horseback existence by Nomads revolutionized human mobility, allowing for extensive migrations.
  • This rapid movement exposed Nomads to diverse microbes, contributing to their overly tolerant immune system.
  • Mounted Nomads quickly encountered new dangerous microbes, leading to specific immune adaptations.
  • Nomads may struggle with low-grade, chronic viral infections if their nitric oxide production is unbalanced.
  • Sluggish immune response in Nomads can lead to persistent fatigue.
  • Some Nomads may experience overactive immune responses, leading to autoimmune diseases like lupus and rheumatoid arthritis.
  • This overactive immune scenario is common in Nomads of African or Asian ancestry and those with specific fingerprint patterns.

Nomad Diet

  • Nomads may have sensitivities to lectins and gluten, making their diet more idiosyncratic.
  • Some Nomads are adapted to fermented dairy products, while others may be lactose intolerant.
  • The adaptation to herding and milking societies allowed Nomads to consume dairy throughout their lives.
  • Signs of adaptation to milking societies include incisor shoveling and smaller teeth.
  • Despite challenges, Nomads possess natural gifts and can thrive with a personalized approach to health.
  • The GenoType Diet recognizes individuality, and Nomads exemplify this uniqueness.

Avoid Foods That

  • Cause hypoglycemia. Since low blood sugar can cause such debilitating fatigue in Nomads, you will want to maintain a diet that keeps your blood sugar levels within a narrow, optimal range.
  • Inhibit your metabolism. Not surprisingly, many of the foods that cause hypoglycemia or that interfere with the proper functioning of nitric oxide in the Nomad also inhibit your metabolism and cause weight gain.
  • Irritate the gut. Many foods contain ingredients that can irritate the lining of the Nomad’s gut, causing fatigue and inflammation. Many mold-containing foods and fungi can cause increased inflammation in Nomads.
  • Contain gluten. Gluten is a protein found in many grains that can irritate the gut lining in sensitive individuals. If you are a Nomad and have discovered that you have white lines on your fingerprints, you’ll want to limit your intake of gluten-containing foods.
